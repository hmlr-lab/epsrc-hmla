EPSRC Sandpit Project

We are a research team working on autonomous systems

We are researchers dedicated to advancing the field of autonomous systems, focusing on developing innovative technologies that enable machines to operate independently with minimal human intervention. Our work involves a multidisciplinary approach, combining expertise in artificial intelligence, robotics, control systems, and data analytics to create systems that can adapt to dynamic environments, make real-time decisions, and learn from their interactions. By pushing the boundaries of automation, we aim to enhance efficiency, safety, and reliability in various applications, from autonomous vehicles and drones to intelligent industrial systems and beyond.

One-Shot Learning of Autonomous Behavior: A Meta-interpretive Learning approach

"One-shot learning" traditionally refers to classifying a single instance using a machine learning model pre-trained on extensive datasets. In contrast, Meta-Interpretive Learning (MIL), a type of Inductive Logic Programming (ILP), can generate complex logic programs from just a single positive example and minimal background knowledge, without prior extensive training. This approach offers a human-centered form of machine learning that is more controllable, reliable, and comprehensible due to its small training data size and the inherent interpretability of logic programs.
